Expression of Interest: Demonstrator Sites

Who We Are

Cwiri focuses on building science-led monitoring, reporting, and verification (MRV) systems that are transparent, scalable, and adaptive to the changing circumstances and requirements for each site. We're a start-up born from the UKCEH peatland science team, founded because we recognised a sector-wide need for robust, scalable, and cost-accessible monitoring solutions.

Our team came together through CivTech Challenge 10.6, working alongside the Woodland Carbon Code and Peatland Code to develop new monitoring and verification solutions that deliver higher assurance than current approaches, apply across different project types, and remain accessible to all projects regardless of budget.

Our Approach

We utilise a tiered approach to monitoring so solutions match your project budget, stage, and assurance requirements. You only pay for what your site needs, and the level of assurance you want. We focus on telemetered sensor deployment and adaptive monitoring that enables sensor relocation over time, improving coverage while keeping resources focused and efficient.

Drawing on decades of combined experience in land-based research, we deliver a layered sensing and reporting workflow that combines low-cost in situ sensors with Earth Observation (drone and satellite) data to provide detailed outputs and recommendations.

Want to Be a Demonstrator Site?

We are now in a 12-month pre-commercial stage with the Woodland Carbon Code and Peatland Code to refine and demonstrate the capability of our solutions. As part of this work, we are seeking early-onboarding demonstrator sites to help validate and refine our modelling, reporting, and verification pipeline.

Selected projects will:

  • Work with us to test the full workflow—from data collection and sensor deployment to modelling outputs and dashboard visualisation
  • Receive free access to our research-grade Earth Observation and modelling outputs during the trial
  • Benefit from in-kind support (negotiated on a per-project basis) to assist with installation, monitoring, and operational costs

If you have a restoration site whose progress you would like to better understand, and an interest in helping develop new monitoring technologies for Peatland and Woodland Carbon Code projects, please complete the Expression of Interest below.

This EOI helps us understand site suitability, logistics, and your capacity to participate in monitoring and co-development activities.
