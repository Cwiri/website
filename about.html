Our Story

Cwiri was formed to solve a practical challenge in nature-based carbon projects: credible monitoring is essential for trust, but often too expensive or too generic to reflect real site conditions. We set out to make monitoring, reporting, and verification more accessible, transparent, and grounded in real evidence.

The company grew out of CivTech Challenge 10.6, in collaboration with the Woodland Carbon Code and Peatland Code. That experience shaped our approach: combine scientific rigour with practical delivery, so projects of different sizes can access robust, adaptive monitoring that supports verification with confidence.

Why We Started

Monitoring Was Too Generic

Many projects needed site-specific evidence, not one-size-fits-all methods.

Cost Barriers Were Too High

Smaller and medium projects needed tiered options that still delivered assurance.

Science Needed Practical Delivery

We bridge cutting-edge peatland and carbon science with field-ready workflows.

Our Approach

We deliver a clear, five-stage workflow: assessment, tier selection, telemetered sensor deployment, QA/QC with analysis, and transparent reporting. The monitoring setup adapts as site conditions and project priorities evolve, helping clients maintain confidence while controlling cost.

Adaptive Monitoring Framework

Our adaptive framework keeps monitoring practical and relevant over time. As projects evolve, we reassess sensor placement, data quality, and analytical priorities, so outputs remain robust without unnecessary cost.
